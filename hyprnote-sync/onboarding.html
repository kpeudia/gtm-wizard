Daily Use

Before a call: Open chex. Click New Note. Click Record.

During the call: It transcribes locally on your device.

After the call: Stop recording. AI generates your notes.

A quick heads-up at the start of calls—"I'm using a note-taking assistant so I can focus on our conversation"—keeps things transparent.

To sync to Salesforce:

Open hyprnote-sync folder. Double-click sync-meetings.command

Do this at end of day. Notes appear as Events on your Accounts.

Security & Privacy

Where is data stored?

Recordings and transcripts stay on your Mac. Nothing is uploaded to external servers.

What about the AI transcription?

Runs locally on your device using on-device speech-to-text. No cloud processing.

What gets sent to Salesforce?

Only the meeting summary text. No audio files, no raw transcripts.

Is this compliant?

Yes. Local-first architecture means customer data never leaves your device. This addresses concerns that exist with cloud-based alternatives.

Troubleshooting

"Cannot be opened because it is from an unidentified developer"

Right-click the file, select "Open", then click "Open" in the popup.

"Hyprnote database not found"

Open chex first and record at least one test note.

"Salesforce connection failed"

Make sure you have the .env file in your hyprnote-sync folder. Ask Keigan.

"npm: command not found"

Node.js isn't installed. Go back to Step 2.

Account not matching correctly?

Add participant emails in chex before recording for better matching.
