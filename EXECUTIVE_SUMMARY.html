GTM-Wizard - Executive Summary

AI-Powered Sales Intelligence Platform

Version: 1.0 Status: Production (Live, serving 41 team members) Launch Date: November 2025

üéØ What Is GTM-Wizard?

GTM-Wizard is an AI-powered conversational interface that sits inside Slack and provides instant access to your Salesforce data. Think of it as having a highly knowledgeable sales operations analyst available 24/7 who can answer any question about your pipeline, accounts, or deals‚Äîinstantly.

In Simple Terms: Instead of logging into Salesforce, navigating multiple screens, building reports, and exporting data, your team simply asks questions in Slack like they would ask a colleague:

  • "Who owns Intel?"
  • "Show me late stage contracting deals"
  • "What's the account plan for Apple?"
  • "What deals were added to pipeline this week?"

GTM-Wizard understands the question, queries Salesforce, and returns a formatted answer in seconds.

Use Case Example: > CFO preparing for board meeting asks "how many ARR customers do we have?" Gets the count AND the full list of account names in 2 seconds. No RevOps request, no waiting, no manual counting.

Use Case Example: > Customer calls with a question about their contract. Instead of putting them on hold while searching Salesforce, CS types "contracts for [Company]", clicks the PDF link, and has the answer in 15 seconds.

What: Structured templates for documenting account strategy: CLO engagement, budget holders, champions, use cases, value prop, timing.

Business Value:

Use Case Example: > New rep takes over Microsoft account. What: Generate formatted Excel reports of pipeline data and upload directly to Slack‚Äîno manual export/format/share workflow.

Use Case Example: > Thursday afternoon, partner asks for current pipeline by product. Instead of exporting from Salesforce, formatting in Excel, and emailing, someone types "send pipeline Excel report" in Slack. Formatted Excel appears in channel in 5 seconds.

What: When GTM-Wizard doesn't understand a question, it asks clarifying questions and suggests relevant capabilities‚Äîno more confusing responses.

  • Learns from what users are trying to ask
  • Graceful degradation instead of wrong answers

Use Case Example: > Rep asks something GTM-Wizard doesn't understand. Instead of getting a random pipeline report, they get: "I noticed you mentioned [keywords]. Are you asking about pipeline data? Account information? Here are examples..." User learns how to phrase questions better.
